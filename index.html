The Game

Welcome to Vice City

Released on October 27, 2002 by Rockstar Games, Grand Theft Auto: Vice City is an open-world action-adventure game set in the fictional Vice City — a sun-soaked metropolis dripping in neon, glamour, and organised crime, inspired by Miami, Florida.

The year is 1986. Tommy Vercetti, fresh out of a 15-year prison stint, is sent south by his mob boss Sonny Forelli to oversee a drug deal. When the deal goes spectacularly wrong in a violent ambush — leaving Tommy with no money and no drugs — he must claw his way from the bottom of the food chain to the very top of the Vice City underworld.

With its razor-sharp writing, unforgettable characters, iconic 80s soundtrack spanning nine radio stations, and a sprawling city to conquer one business at a time, Vice City is widely regarded as one of the greatest video games ever made — a love letter to a decade of excess, ambition, and crime.

The Cast

Faces of Vice City

Protagonist

Tommy Vercetti

A ruthless Liberty City mobster sent to Vice City after 15 years in prison. Determined, cunning, and capable of extraordinary violence — Tommy didn't just survive Vice City, he conquered it. Voiced by Ray Liotta.

Ally · Traitor

Lance Vance

A flashy, white-suited dealer who teams up with Tommy after the drug deal ambush. Charming but ultimately untrustworthy. Voiced by Philip Michael Thomas of Miami Vice fame.

Ally · Lawyer

Ken Rosenberg

Tommy's neurotic, sweating, cocaine-addled attorney. Equal parts comic relief and genuine help, Ken Rosenberg is the kind of man who pokes his head out of the gutter for one second and fate shovels dirt in his face. Voiced by William Fichtner.

Antagonist · Drug Lord

Ricardo Diaz

The volatile, paranoid cocaine kingpin who orchestrated the ambush that set the whole story in motion. His mansion on Starfish Island becomes Tommy's greatest trophy. Voiced by Luis Guzmán.

S
Antagonist · Mob Boss

Sonny Forelli

Tommy's former Liberty City boss who sent him south — and never stopped reminding him of debts owed. Ruthless, calculating, and totally convinced he owns Tommy. Voiced by Tom Sizemore.

C
Ally · Mentor

Colonel Cortez

A retired army colonel and arms dealer who takes Tommy under his wing early in the story. Elegant, well-connected, and sharp enough to know when it's time to sail away. Voiced by Robert Davi.

Did You Know?

Vice City Trivia

🎬

Tommy Vercetti is voiced by Ray Liotta, known for his role as Henry Hill in Goodfellas — a clear nod to the gangster film influences behind the character.

🌴

Vice City is heavily inspired by Miami in 1986 — a city gripped by the cocaine trade, depicted unflinchingly in Brian De Palma's Scarface (1983) and the TV show Miami Vice.

📺

Lance Vance is voiced by Philip Michael Thomas, who played Detective Ricardo Tubbs in Miami Vice — making him a direct reference to one of Vice City's biggest inspirations.

🏢

By the end of the game Tommy can own 14 properties across Vice City, including the Malibu Club, Pole Position Club, the Cherry Popper Ice Cream factory, and the Vercetti Estate.

🎸

The game features 103 licensed songs from real 1980s artists, including Duran Duran, Michael Jackson, Ozzy Osbourne, Blondie, and Miami Sound Machine — making the soundtrack arguably the best in any video game.

🚁

Vice City was the first GTA game to feature helicopters as controllable vehicles. The Bell 206 (re-named "Maverick") became iconic and appeared in every GTA since.

💰

The game sold over 17.5 million copies and was the best-selling video game of 2002 worldwide. It broke records for fastest-selling game at the time of release.

🏆

Tommy Vercetti is the only GTA protagonist to permanently own the city by the end of the main storyline — sitting at the top of the Vercetti Estate as the undisputed boss of Vice City.
